Depression: a common and serious mental ilness that negavitvely affects a person's way of thinking, feelings and habits. Furthermore it causes the feeling of sadness and/or loss of interest in activities that a person previously found joy in.


In the United States the leading cause of disability for people between the ages of 15 and 44 is Major Depressive Disorder.

Anxiety: a normal reaction to stress.
Anxiety disorders: a group od mental illnesses that cause persistent and overpowering anxiety and fear. The prolonged and excessive feeling of anxiety can heavily affect people's day-to-day activities and can be worsened with triggers.


Anxiety disorders are the most common mental illnesses in the United States, affecting approximately 18.1% of the population (age 18 and older).

Substance abuse: a complex condition in which there is uncontrolled use of a substance despite harmful repurcussions. People's lives are heavily impacted and sometimes their ability to function becomes impaired due to their intentense focus and in reliance in a specific substance(s)


It is estimated that around 21 million Americans have at least 1 addiction, however only 10% of that group receive treatment.

HELPING YOURSELF


  • Appreciate yourself

    • - try to treat yourself with dignity and kindness.

  • Take care of your body

    • - taking care of your body can improve your mental health. This includes eating properly, exercising regurarly and sleeping well.

  • Be involved with supportive people

    • - interact with family and friends that provide a good support system.

  • Volunteer

    • - give your time and energy to other peole without expecting anything in return.

  • Learn to destress

    • - stress is a vital emotion. However learning good coping mechanisms to level the head is equally vital.

  • Control overthinking

    • - quieting the mind can help the feelings of anxiety and clear the head.

  • Setting attainable goals

    • - whether academically, professionally or personally it is important to have an endgame. As important as it is to aim high it is
    equally important to be realistic.

  • Take small adventures

    • - having a routine is effective and efficient but can get stale. Doing something different can be a breathe of fresh air.

  • Avoid harmful substances

    • - a common misconception is that using harmful substances alleviates problems. Most times it aggravtes them instead.

  • Reach out

    • - getting help when you need it is not a weakness. It is a strength.

HELPING OTHERS


  • Educating yourself

    • - learning about mental health conditions can help understanding others better.

  • Avoid stigmatization

    • - mental health conditoins should be treated with the same respect as other health conditions.

  • Lend an ear and learn

    • - people are not their disorder or symptoms, so listen to their life experiences.

  • Help to find help

    • - when others are feeling to anxious assist them in taking the first sfew steps.

  • Extend meaningful support

    • - when offering to be support, provide actaul assistance.

  • Take symptoms earnestly

    • - mental health isuues changes a person's perspective so it's important to remember mental health symptoms are real symptoms.

  • Don't overstep

    • - trying to force people into treatment is not only ineffective but will also demean their agency and strength.

  • Display unconditional love

    • - often people with mental health issues feel unloved. Reassure them that they are loved.

  • strengthen both strengths and weaknesses

    • - celebrate small victories together.

  • Take care of yourself

    • - you can't help others if you are unwell yourself.
